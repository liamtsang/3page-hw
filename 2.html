Details

Every 10 years, the amount of electors allocated to each state is recalculated based on census data. Each state has different methods in picking their electors, but most are chosen by the state's winning party. Electors sometimes will vote for a candidate that their state didn't vote for (7 electors did this in 2016).

Electors get one vote for President and one vote for Vice President, and the result of these votes is what ultimately decides the winner.
